This is the complete FairFlow Guardian walkthrough for the BGA AI Trading and Strategy Track.

The project is not trying to win a pure profit contest. It is a trading safety layer that asks a better question: is this market fair enough for a retail user to participate?

The walkthrough starts with the judge-facing thesis, readiness score, current audit, and route proof. From there, it explains the live dashboard and the two most important demo paths.

In the calm path, the system may approve a small paper trade, but only after checking liquidity, spread, volatility, hidden costs, liquidation stress, manipulation signals, and retail fairness.

In the manipulated path, the same strategy is rejected. FairFlow blocks the trade, locks the execution route, and explains why no trade is the healthier market action.

The middle of the walkthrough shows what makes the project agentic. A strategy agent proposes, but risk, manipulation, execution, cohort fairness, policy stress, red-team, and audit agents can challenge or block the action.

The evidence pack turns the decision into something judges can inspect. It includes the receipt, guardrail failures, route analysis, policy stress results, cohort impact, red-team probes, and an on-chain anchor payload.

The final message is simple. FairFlow reduces information asymmetry, protects smaller retail accounts, and makes automated trading decisions easier to verify and challenge.

It is built around better systems, not bigger bets.
