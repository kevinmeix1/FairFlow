FairFlow Guardian is an AI trading safety layer for the BGA AI Trading and Strategy Track.

The thesis is simple: a better trading system should not just chase returns. It should know when the market is fair enough for retail users, and when no trade is the safer answer.

The demo starts on the dashboard. Judges see live market context, active agents, readiness, route proof, and a verifiable audit hash.

In calm conditions, FairFlow allows only a small, risk-capped paper trade. That approval is conditional. The strategy is checked against liquidity, spread, volatility, hidden cost, liquidation stress, manipulation signals, and retail fairness.

Then the demo switches to the manipulated path. The asset is the same, but market integrity is lower. FairFlow blocks the trade, locks execution, and explains which guardrails failed.

That is the key product choice. The agent is not rewarded for forcing action. It is rewarded for refusing unsafe automation.

The evidence pack makes the decision inspectable. Each audit includes the receipt, agent concerns, policy stress result, red-team probes, cohort fairness check, and on-chain anchor payload.

FairFlow aligns with blockchain-for-good by reducing information asymmetry, protecting smaller accounts, making risk visible, and keeping decisions open to challenge.

The strongest claim is not that it predicts every market. The claim is that it makes trading automation safer, fairer, and more accountable.
